§ 1 · What you get

Determinations with reasoning attached.

Send facts about a person's situation. Get back, for every applicable law: eligibility, entitlement, the computed benefit — and the authority behind each conclusion.

Justification trees

Every conclusion is a finding tied to a citation. When the answer is no, you see exactly which condition failed — and which still passed.

✓ 12 months' employment ✗ 1,250 hours of service hours in last 12 mo: 900 [29 U.S.C. § 2611(2)(A)(ii)]

Effective-date time travel

Every rate and threshold is effective-dated. Evaluate any determination under the law as of any date — for audits, claims, or pricing next year today.

as_of=2025-06-01 benefit: 1177.32 as_of=2026-09-01 benefit: 1232.36 as_of=2027-02-01 benefit: 1289.84

Honest about judgment

Open-textured law — "serious health condition" — is never resolved by the machine. It's flagged and routed to a person, with the regulation attached.

eligible: null ⚖ "serious health condition" requires medical certification [29 C.F.R. § 825.113]
§ 2 · The maintenance loop

Amendments become reviewed diffs, not projects.

Legislatures move; the encoding follows — through three gates.

// NYSDOL notice: 2027 state average weekly wage ny.saww 2026-01-01 $1,839.34 ny.saww + 2027-01-01 $1,925.14 “Effective January 1, 2027 …”
i
An LLM drafts.

Watches legislative and agency feeds; drafts parameter diffs with the source quoted verbatim. Rule-structure changes always route to a human encoder.

ii
The regression suite gates.

Every diff runs against pinned historic determinations. Hallucinated keys and rewritten history fail closed.

iii
An attorney signs.

Nothing ships without recorded human approval. Source hash, model, and reviewer travel with every change — and your systems get the webhook.

§ 3 · Open core

The code is free. Being current is the product.

The encodings and their test suites are open source — audit every line against the statute. What you subscribe to is the guarantee: maintained coverage under an SLA, effective-date history, and amendment alerts that tell you which of your open leaves a change affects.

Today the prototype covers FMLA plus California, Massachusetts, Minnesota, New Jersey, New York, and Washington — eight regimes across six states, with a 94-scenario regression suite and the amendment pipeline running end to end.

  • Payroll / HRIS / EOR platforms — replace an internal rules team with an API line-item
  • Leave-management platforms — shed your largest non-differentiating cost
  • Insurers & TPAs — determinations at claims scale, with audit trails
  • AI copilots — a citation-backed oracle behind the chat, via MCP
