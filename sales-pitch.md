# OpenLeave — The Sales Pitch

**Every leave decision, backed by the letter of the law.**

---

Thirteen states and D.C. now run paid family leave programs — each with its own eligibility tests, benefit formulas, and annual rate changes. Minnesota and Delaware just went live. Maryland and Virginia are next. One remote employee in a state puts you on the hook for that state's rules.

Today, every HR platform, payroll provider, and insurer solves this the same way: a team of analysts reads the statutes and hand-codes the rules into a black box. Every vendor pays for that team. Every vendor's black box disagrees with the others. And every January, they all scramble to re-code the same rate changes at the same time.

**OpenLeave is the rules layer, sold as an API.**

Send us a fact pattern — where the employee works, their hours, their wage, the leave event. We return the determination for every applicable law: eligible or not, how many weeks, job-protected or not, the weekly benefit to the cent, and how the leaves interact. Every conclusion cites the statute that produced it — `29 U.S.C. § 2611`, not "because the system said so."

Three things nobody else gives you:

- **A justification tree, not a verdict.** When your customer's employee is denied leave, you can show exactly which condition failed and the citation behind it. Auditors and plaintiffs' counsel stop being scary.
- **Time travel.** Evaluate any determination under the law as it stood on any date. Reconstruct a 2026 decision in a 2028 audit in one API call.
- **Rules that stay current.** Our AI pipeline watches every legislature and agency feed, drafts the encoding change the day a rate notice drops, proves it against a regression suite of pinned determinations, and ships it after attorney sign-off. You get a webhook: "this change affects 14 of your open leaves."

The encodings are open source — audit every line against the statute. What you pay for is the guarantee: maintained coverage, an SLA, effective-date history, and change alerts.

Your engineers stop transcribing statutes. Your compliance costs stop scaling with the statute count. And when the next state launches its program, your product supports it on day one — because we already do.

**Stop re-coding the law. Start calling it.**
