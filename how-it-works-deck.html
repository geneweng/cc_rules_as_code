OpenLeave — How It WorksRaw Rules
§ 1Raw Rules
Thresholds, date arithmetic, a piecewise formula — closed-texture law, the kind that encodes faithfully.

What the law says.

The running example: a Minnesotan hired March 2025, full time at $1,100/week, 85-person employer, bonding leave starting September 1, 2026. Two rules govern it.

"The term 'eligible employee' means an employee who has been employed — (i) for at least 12 months by the employer…; and (ii) for at least 1,250 hours of service with such employer during the previous 12-month period… [excluding] any employee… at a worksite at which such employer employs less than 50 employees [within] 75 miles…" 29 U.S.C. § 2611(2) — Family and Medical Leave Act (excerpt)
"The weekly benefit amount is 90% of wages that do not exceed 50% of the state's average weekly wage, plus 66% of wages between 50% and 100% of that wage… capped at the state average weekly wage. Nearly all employers are covered, regardless of size." Minn. Stat. ch. 268B — Minnesota Paid Leave (summarized excerpt)

§ 2continued
Rates and caps are never hard-coded: effective-dated parameters enable “the law as of any date” — and are what the AI amendment pipeline maintains.

Formulas transcribe; numbers stay data.

The § 268B.06 benefit formula — a direct transcription of the statute's piecewise structure
def _weekly_benefit(aww, saww): # Minn. Stat. § 268B.06 half = 0.5 * saww benefit = 0.9 * min(aww, half) # 90% up to 50% of SAWW if aww > half: benefit += 0.66 * (min(aww, saww) - half) # 66% between 50–100% if aww > saww: benefit += 0.55 * (aww - saww) # 55% above return round(min(benefit, saww), 2) # capped at SAWW

§ 3Fact Input
The system — not the user — figures out which laws apply.

What the user tells the system.

The caller — an HR platform, a payroll system, a chatbot, or a person using the web checker — submits only facts. No legal knowledge required to ask.

POST /determinations { "facts": { "employee": { "work_state": "MN", ← where they work "hire_date": "2025-03-01", ← when they were hired "hours_last_12mo": 1400, ← how much they work "average_weekly_wage": 1100 ← what they earn }, "employer": { "total_employees": 85 }, ← how big the employer is "event": { "type": "bonding", "start": "2026-09-01" } ← what's happening } }

When the answer is no, the tree shows exactly which condition failed — and which still passed.
